To start out, find a collection of the kanji and study each one for a minute. Do NOT put pressure on yourself to retain it immediately. It'll take at least 3 of these to solidify the memory. Be sure to visualize the meaning in association with the character, make up a story about it, and incorporate as many senses as possible. An example of sensory learning could be to sniff a thing while reading the character, take a bite of something while thinking of the meaning, and listen to how you say it aloud.

Keep in mind, there is no ONE and only way to pronounce a kanji. The context of the character in sentence can change the pronunciation and effect it's meaning. Just familiarize yourself with it, because the memorization part will come along with learning the vocabulary where the kanji and hiragana mix.

Try memorizing the vocabulary and learning the kanji alongside that so that you are actually learning words too. I have noticed many people focus so hard on kanji memorization, and falter on actual words even though they know hiragana. Comprehension is a delicate balance.

Use [WaniKani](https://www.wanikani.com/dashboard) to help study Radicals, Kanji, & Vocab. Use [This YouTube Course](https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PLVGw_A21plC4CNCpCAU_6j7eYcRSjvvyP&si=7s8SGW3fw9Szi-hd) to guide your learning of Kanji.

Method of Learning Individual Kanji:

- Use [Jisho.org](https://jisho.org/) for Definitions & Audio.
- Use [WaniKani](https://www.wanikani.com/) for Cross Reference (Pink for Kanji).
- Use [Kakimashou](https://www.kakimashou.com/) to Learn Writing.
- Practice Saying Each Kanji Out Loud, Individually & In Sentence.

- 音読み (on'yomi reading : Chinese Origin)
- 訓読み (kun'yomi reading : Japanese Origin)
